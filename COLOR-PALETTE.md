# Study Abroad (Pvt) Ltd — Website Colour Palette

A quick guide to the colours used across the website, what each one is for, and
where you can see it live on the site.

---

## Core colours

### Primary Blue — `#135ABE`
The main brand colour and the colour of action. Used for anything we want people
to click or notice.
**On the site:** the "Free Consultation" and "Contact Us" buttons, text links like
"Read more", the small line above each section title, and the service icons.

### Brand Navy — `#123D6E` (deep) / `#0B2746` (deepest)
The darker, premium tone used for strong, trust-building areas.
**On the site:** the dark band at the top of inner pages (e.g. About Us, Services),
the statistics strip ("19+ Years", "99% Visa Success"), the top of the testimonial
cards, the hero background, and the footer.

### Accent Gold — `#F5A524`
A warm highlight used sparingly for credibility cues — never for large areas.
**On the site:** the star ratings on student testimonials.

---

## Backgrounds & text

### Page Background — White `#FFFFFF`
The clean base of every page that keeps the design light and modern.
**On the site:** the main background behind most sections.

### Soft Blue Background — `#EAF2FE`
A very light blue used to gently separate alternating sections so the page has rhythm.
**On the site:** the "Book Your Free Consultation" area and the "What Sets Us Apart" section.

### Ink (Headings) — `#0F172A`
Near-black used for headings, so titles read strong and clear.
**On the site:** every section heading, e.g. "Your gateway to world-class education".

### Body Text — `#334155`
A soft dark grey for paragraphs — easier on the eyes than pure black.
**On the site:** all the descriptive paragraph text across the site.

---

## Status colours (forms & messages)

These appear only in feedback messages, mostly on the consultation form.

### Success — `#16A34A` (green)
Confirms something worked.
**On the site:** the "thank you / submitted successfully" message after the consultation form is sent.

### Warning — `#D97706` (amber)
Flags something that needs attention but isn't an error.
**On the site:** cautionary notices in forms (e.g. an incomplete optional step).

### Error — `#DC2626` (red)
Shows something went wrong and must be fixed.
**On the site:** form validation messages (e.g. "please enter a valid email") on the consultation form.

---

### In one line
Action = **Blue `#135ABE`**, trust/depth = **Navy `#123D6E`**, highlight = **Gold `#F5A524`**,
on a clean **white** page with **near-black headings** and **soft-grey body text** — plus
standard green / amber / red for form messages.
